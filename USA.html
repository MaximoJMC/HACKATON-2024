Getting to know the United States

United Statess

The United States is known for its great cultural, economic, and political influence in the world. However, it faces significant challenges in terms of climate change, including rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and air pollution. Dependence on fossil fuels and deforestation are critical issues that must be addressed to mitigate its environmental impact.

Forest Fires in 2024

In 2024, wildfires in the United States have reached alarming levels, burning more than 6 million acres. Extreme heat waves and prolonged droughts have created perfect conditions for these fires to spread. California and Oregon have been particularly hard hit, with thousands of homes evacuated and communities destroyed.

Según la National Interagency Fire Center, The number of forest fires has increased by 30% compared to the average of the last five years. This increase is directly attributed to climate change and inadequate forest management..

Carbon Dioxide Pollution

CO2 emissions in the United States were approximately 5.1 billion tons in 2024. Transportation and electricity generation are the main sources of these emissions, accounting for 29% and 25% respectively, according to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).

The use of fossil fuels remains predominant, although renewable energy is on the rise. The current administration has implemented policies to reduce these emissions, such as incentives for electric vehicles and the expansion of solar and wind energy.

Desafíos del Cambio Climático

Desde 1880, la temperatura media de Estados Unidos ha aumentado casi 2 °F (1.1 °C), contribuyendo a fenómenos climáticos extremos como huracanes, sequías y olas de calor. Estos eventos no solo afectan el medio ambiente, sino que también tienen un costo económico significativo, con daños estimados en miles de millones de dólares cada año.

Climate Change Challenges

Since 1880, the average temperature in the United States has increased by nearly 2 °F (1.1 °C), contributing to extreme weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and heat waves. These events not only affect the environment but also have a significant economic cost, with estimated damages in the billions of dollars each year.

Sustainable Transportation

The development of electric vehicles and sustainable public transportation systems are key steps in reducing emissions.

Renewable Energy

Solar and wind energy are gaining traction as alternative sources to fossil fuels.

Energy Efficiency

Improving the energy efficiency of buildings and industries is essential for reducing the carbon footprint.
