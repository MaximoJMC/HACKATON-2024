Getting to Know Our Countries

Peru

Peru is famous for its rich biodiversity and cultural heritage. However, the country faces serious threats due to climate change, such as glacier melting, which affects water supply, and extreme weather events that impact agriculture and food security.

Wildfires in 2024

In 2024, Peru has experienced an alarming increase in the frequency and intensity of wildfires. These fires not only destroy vast areas of forest but also release large amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2) into the atmosphere, exacerbating global warming. It is estimated that the fires this year have released more than 10 million tons of CO2, affecting air quality and public health.

Departments with the Most Heat

Since 1880, global temperatures have increased significantly, and Peru is no exception. In 2024, some regions have reached record temperatures, contributing to an increase in wildfires and other extreme weather events.

Coast

The Peruvian coast is known for its temperate climate and fishing resources but faces challenges from rising sea levels.

Sierra

The Peruvian sierra has a varied climate, but climate change is causing more severe droughts and changes in agriculture.

Jungle

The Peruvian jungle, rich in biodiversity, is threatened by deforestation and wildfires.

WILDFIRES

Wildfires in Peru have drastically increased in 2024, affecting biodiversity and the health of local communities.

These fires are driven by drought, rising temperatures, and deforestation.

Mitigation actions are essential to curb the rise of CO2 emissions and protect our ecosystems.

Reforestation and sustainable management initiatives are being implemented to address this crisis.
